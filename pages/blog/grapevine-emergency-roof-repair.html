Emergency Roof Repair in Grapevine, TX: What to Do After a Storm

Published April 13, 2026 — By SkyGuard Roofing Solutions

Grapevine sits in the heart of North Texas's most active storm corridor. Every spring, supercell thunderstorms track across Tarrant and Dallas counties, bringing hail, straight-line winds exceeding 70 mph, and occasional tornadoes. When a severe storm damages your roof, the hours immediately following are critical — both for minimizing further damage to your home and for protecting your insurance claim. This guide walks through exactly what to do, step by step.

Step 1: Ensure Everyone Is Safe

Before you assess any property damage, confirm that every person in your household is safe. If a tree has fallen onto the roof or a portion of the structure has collapsed, do not enter affected rooms until a professional has determined the structural integrity is sound. Downed power lines near the home require calling 911 and keeping distance — never attempt to move a power line yourself. Once safety is confirmed, document your condition and the time of the event, as this information matters for your insurance claim timeline.

Step 2: Prevent Further Water Intrusion

If rain is still falling or more precipitation is forecast, your priority is stopping water from entering through any breaches in the roof. Place buckets or bins beneath active drips inside the home. Move valuables, electronics, and documents away from wet areas. If you have a ladder and can safely access the exterior from ground level without climbing onto a wet, potentially unstable roof, visually locate the breach. Otherwise, wait for conditions to improve or call an emergency contractor who carries fall protection equipment.

Step 3: Apply Emergency Tarping

Emergency roof tarps are the standard immediate remedy for storm-caused breaches. A heavy-duty polyethylene tarp (6 mil or heavier) should extend at least four feet beyond the damaged area on all sides and be secured with furring strips or sandbags along the edges — not staples or nails through the tarp alone, which create additional penetration points. Tarping is not a permanent repair; it buys time while the insurance process unfolds and materials are ordered. Step 4: Document Everything Before Cleanup

This step is critically important for your insurance claim. Before any debris is cleared, photograph and video every aspect of the damage: exterior roof from every angle, interior ceilings with any water staining, damaged personal property, and the condition of gutters, siding, and windows. Note the date and time on each photo if your phone's camera metadata does not capture it automatically. Do not discard any damaged materials — adjusters often want to inspect dislodged shingles, cracked solar panels, or dented gutters to validate the scope of the claim.

Step 5: Contact Your Insurance Company

File your claim as soon as practically possible. Texas homeowner policies generally require claims to be filed within one year of the date of loss, but earlier is always better — both for scheduling an adjuster visit promptly and for avoiding questions about whether damage is pre-existing. When you call, have your policy number, the date of the event, and a summary of the visible damage ready. Ask specifically whether your policy includes ACV (actual cash value) or RCV (replacement cost value) coverage, as this affects how your settlement is calculated.

Step 6: Choose a Local, Licensed Contractor — Not a Storm Chaser

After every significant hail event in the DFW area, out-of-state roofing contractors flood neighborhoods offering quick estimates. These storm chasers frequently disappear after collecting deposits, use substandard materials, skip permits, and leave homeowners with improperly installed roofs and voided manufacturer warranties. Protect yourself by working with a licensed Texas roofing contractor with a verifiable local address and established reputation. Verify the contractor's license on the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation website, check their Better Business Bureau rating, and ask for references from recent Grapevine or Tarrant County jobs.

What Legitimate Emergency Contractors Do

A reputable emergency roofing contractor will inspect your roof thoroughly, provide written documentation of all damage found, advise on whether repair or full replacement is appropriate, assist you in preparing documentation for your insurance adjuster, and pull the required permits before beginning work. They will not pressure you to sign a contract before you have received your insurance settlement, nor will they ask for the full payment upfront.
