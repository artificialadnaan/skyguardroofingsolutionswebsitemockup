Metal Roofing vs. Shingles in Denton, TX: Which Is Right for Your Home?

Published March 30, 2026 — By SkyGuard Roofing Solutions

When it is time to replace a roof in Denton, Texas, homeowners face a fundamental choice: stick with traditional asphalt shingles or invest in a metal roofing system. Both are viable options, but they perform differently in North Texas's climate and carry very different cost profiles over time. This guide breaks down the comparison across every dimension that matters, so you can make a confident, informed decision for your home.

Upfront Cost: Shingles Win in the Short Term

There is no avoiding the price gap at installation. Architectural asphalt shingles in Denton typically run $4 to $7 per square foot installed, including underlayment, flashing, and labor. A standing-seam metal roof on the same home will cost $10 to $18 per square foot installed, depending on the metal gauge, panel profile, and complexity of the roof geometry. On a 2,000-square-foot home with a 2,500-square-foot roof surface, that difference can be $15,000 to $25,000. For homeowners on a fixed timeline or budget, shingles remain the more accessible entry point. Flexible financing options can make metal roofing attainable without depleting savings.

Lifespan: Metal Roofing Pulls Ahead Significantly

In Denton's climate, architectural shingles realistically last 20 to 25 years with proper maintenance. Premium shingles rated for 30 years often fall short of that in Texas due to UV degradation and thermal cycling — the expansion and contraction from hot days to cool nights that stresses the shingle mat over time. Metal roofing, by contrast, carries manufacturer warranties of 40 to 50 years and routinely lasts 60 years or more with basic maintenance. When you factor in that a metal roof may outlast two or three shingle replacements over the same period, the true lifetime cost comparison narrows considerably.

Hail Resistance: A Critical Factor for North Texas

Denton County sits within the hail corridor that sweeps through North Texas every spring. Hailstorms producing stones one inch in diameter or larger are a near-annual occurrence. Impact resistance is rated on a Class 1 through Class 4 scale. Most standard shingles are Class 3 at best. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles are available and worth the upgrade in this region. Metal roofing — particularly steel rated at 26 gauge or heavier — achieves Class 4 ratings and resists denting from all but the largest hail events. Some insurance carriers in Texas offer premium discounts of 10 to 30 percent for Class 4 rated roofing materials, which partially offsets the higher installation cost.

Energy Efficiency: Metal Keeps Your Home Cooler

During Denton summers, roof surface temperatures can exceed 160°F on a dark asphalt shingle roof. Metal roofing with a reflective finish or cool-roof coating reflects a significantly higher percentage of solar radiation, keeping surface temperatures 50 to 70 degrees lower. This directly reduces the radiant heat load entering your attic, cutting air conditioning demand during the months when electricity costs peak. Properly vented metal roofing systems combined with attic insulation can reduce cooling costs by 15 to 25 percent annually. Shingles can be purchased in lighter colors to improve reflectivity, but they do not match the thermal performance of bare or coated metal panels.

Aesthetics and Noise: Addressing Common Concerns

Metal roofing has evolved far beyond corrugated barn panels. Modern standing-seam and metal shake profiles offer clean, contemporary lines that complement craftsman, ranch, and modern farmhouse architectural styles common throughout Denton's neighborhoods. Metal tiles that mimic clay tile or wood shake are also available for traditional looks. On the noise question — a concern often raised — properly installed metal roofing over solid decking with underlayment is no louder during rain than asphalt shingles. The noise issue primarily affects pole barns and structures where metal is installed over open framing without decking or insulation.

Resale Value

According to Remodeling Magazine's Cost vs. Value reports, metal roofing consistently returns 60 to 70 percent of its installation cost at resale. In a competitive North Texas real estate market where buyers are increasingly aware of long-term ownership costs, a newer metal roof is a meaningful differentiator. Asphalt shingles return a similar percentage of their lower installation cost. The practical advantage with metal is that a roof installed today will still be warrantied and in excellent condition 25 years from now when the property sells — no need for the buyer to immediately budget a replacement.

Which Should You Choose?

If you plan to stay in your Denton home for 10 years or more and have the budget — or can utilize financing — metal roofing offers the stronger long-term value proposition for North Texas. The combination of longevity, hail resistance, energy savings, and insurance premium reductions builds a compelling financial case over time. If your priority is the lowest upfront cost and you expect to sell within the next decade, premium architectural shingles with a Class 4 impact-resistance rating are a sensible, cost-effective choice.
