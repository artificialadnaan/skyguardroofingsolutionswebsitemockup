Lewisville, TX Roof Replacement Timeline: What to Expect

Published June 15, 2026 • By SkyGuard Roofing Solutions

For most Lewisville homeowners, a roof replacement is one of the largest single home improvement investments they will ever make. It is also one of the least familiar processes — most people go through it only once or twice in their lifetime. Not knowing what to expect creates anxiety, makes it harder to evaluate contractor proposals, and leaves homeowners vulnerable to surprises. This guide walks through every stage of a typical residential roof replacement in Lewisville so you know exactly what is happening and why.

Stage One: Initial Inspection (Week One)

The process begins with a thorough inspection of your existing roof system. A qualified contractor will examine the shingles, flashings, gutters, fascia, soffit, ventilation, and attic from below. The inspection determines whether repair is sufficient or replacement is warranted, identifies any decking damage that will need to be addressed during installation, and establishes the scope of work for your proposal.

In Lewisville, the City of Lewisville Building Inspections Department requires a permit for roof replacements. A reputable contractor pulls this permit on your behalf before work begins — never work with a contractor who skips the permit, as it creates liability and can affect your home's resale value. We handle permitting for all residential roofing projects as a standard part of our process.

Stage Two: Proposal, Contract, and Material Selection (Week Two)

After the inspection, you receive a written proposal detailing the scope of work, materials specified, timeline, payment terms, and warranty information. Take time to review this carefully. The proposal should specify the exact shingle product and color, underlayment type, ice-and-water shield application areas, ventilation work included, and what happens if additional decking damage is discovered during tear-off.

Choosing Your Materials

For most Lewisville homes, architectural asphalt shingles are the most practical and cost-effective choice. Products in the 30-year warranty range offer a strong balance of durability, appearance, and cost. Impact-resistant Class 4 shingles cost more upfront but may qualify your home for insurance premium discounts and provide meaningful added hail protection. If you are weighing material options, our team will walk you through the trade-offs without pressure. Review financing options if the material upgrade is a concern.

Stage Three: Permit and Material Ordering (Week Two to Three)

Once you sign the contract, the contractor submits for a building permit with the City of Lewisville and orders materials from the supplier. Standard architectural shingles are typically available within a few days. Specialty products — designer shingles, metal components, or specialty underlayments — may take one to two weeks to arrive. Your installation date is scheduled based on permit approval, material delivery, and crew availability.

Stage Four: Weather Delays

North Texas weather is unpredictable, and roofing work cannot safely proceed in rain, high wind, or extreme heat conditions that exceed safe working parameters. Most contractors build a buffer into their scheduling, but weather delays are sometimes unavoidable. A two-to-four-week installation window is typical in the DFW market. Communicate with your contractor if you have time-sensitive needs, such as a home sale or insurance deadline, so they can prioritize accordingly.

Stage Five: Installation Day

A full residential roof replacement in Lewisville typically takes one to two days for a standard home. Larger or more complex rooflines may take two to three days. Here is what to expect on installation day.

  • Crew arrives early morning, typically between 7:00 and 8:00 a.m.
  • Protection is placed around landscaping, AC units, and pool equipment
  • Existing shingles and underlayment are torn off and loaded into a dump trailer
  • Decking is inspected; damaged boards are replaced at the contracted rate
  • Ice-and-water shield is applied at eaves, valleys, and penetrations
  • Underlayment is installed across the field of the roof
  • New shingles are nailed in courses from eave to ridge
  • Ridge cap and all flashings are installed
  • Magnetic roller sweeps the yard for nails before crew departs

Stage Six: Cleanup and Final Inspection

On completion day, the crew performs a thorough cleanup of all debris. A magnetic roller is used to collect fasteners from the yard, driveway, and landscaping areas. The contractor does a final walkthrough with you to verify the installation quality, review warranty documentation, and address any questions. The city inspector then performs a final inspection to confirm code compliance — this typically takes one to three days to schedule.

Total elapsed time from initial inspection to completion for a typical Lewisville replacement is three to six weeks under normal conditions. Post-storm season may be longer due to high demand.
