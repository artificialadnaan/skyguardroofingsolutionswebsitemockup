Roof Inspection Checklist for Weatherford, TX Homeowners

Published April 6, 2026 — By SkyGuard Roofing Solutions

Parker County homeowners in Weatherford face a combination of hailstorms, high winds, and temperature swings that put significant stress on residential roofing systems year after year. A proactive inspection approach — twice a year at minimum, and after every major storm — is the most effective way to catch problems before they escalate into costly repairs or full replacements. This checklist walks through what you can safely observe from ground level and what to look for in your attic, followed by guidance on when a professional roof inspection is the appropriate next step.

Safety First: What You Should and Should Not Do Yourself

Walking on a residential roof without proper equipment and experience is genuinely dangerous, particularly on steep-pitched homes common in Weatherford's newer subdivisions. For most homeowners, the safest approach is to conduct ground-level observations using binoculars, supplemented by an attic inspection from inside. Leave physical on-roof inspection to licensed contractors with appropriate fall protection. This checklist reflects that boundary.

Part 1: Exterior Ground-Level Checklist

Shingle Condition

Walk the perimeter of your home with binoculars on a clear day. Look for shingles that are curling at the edges or lifting in the middle. Note any sections where shingles appear darker than surrounding areas — this often indicates granule loss that has exposed the underlying asphalt mat. Missing shingles create immediate entry points for water and should be addressed without delay.

Ridge and Hip Caps

The ridge cap runs along the peak of your roof and is the most exposed point on the entire surface. Check that cap shingles are fully adhered, not cracked or missing. Weatherford's wind events frequently dislodge ridge caps before any other shingles fail.

Flashing at Penetrations

Visually inspect the metal flashing around chimneys, skylights, vent pipes, and HVAC equipment that penetrates the roof. Flashing should lie flat with no visible gaps, rust, or lifted sections. Even small separations in flashing allow water to channel directly onto the decking.

Gutters and Downspouts

Check gutters for granule accumulation — a sign of accelerated shingle wear. Gutters should be securely fastened, free of sagging sections, and clear of debris. Downspouts should direct water at least four feet away from the foundation. Clogged gutters cause water to back up under the drip edge and rot fascia boards.

Soffit and Fascia

The soffit panels beneath the roof overhang and the fascia boards at the eave should show no signs of rot, staining, or pest damage. Staining on these surfaces frequently traces back to a gutter or flashing problem above.

Roof Deck Profile

Stand at each corner of your home and sight down the roofline. It should appear straight and even. Any dips, waves, or sagging sections indicate structural problems with the decking or framing below — conditions requiring immediate professional evaluation.

Part 2: Interior Attic Checklist

Daylight Penetration

On a bright day, enter your attic and let your eyes adjust. Any visible light coming through the roof boards (other than through vents) indicates gaps that allow water intrusion. Mark these locations and include them in your professional inspection report.

Water Stains and Moisture

Look for dark staining, water rings, or wet spots on the sheathing, rafters, and insulation. Even dried stains indicate that water has entered at some point. Active moisture or mold growth requires immediate action.

Ventilation Adequacy

A properly ventilated attic should feel close in temperature to the outside air (within reason). An attic that is dramatically hotter than outside suggests insufficient airflow — a condition that accelerates shingle degradation from below and raises cooling costs. Check that soffit vents are not blocked by insulation and that ridge vents or power vents are present and functional.

Insulation Condition

Insulation that is wet, matted, or discolored has been compromised by moisture. Wet insulation loses its R-value and creates an environment for mold growth. Inspect the entire attic floor systematically.

When to Schedule a Professional Inspection

A DIY visual inspection is a valuable first step, but it has clear limitations. Professional inspectors physically access the roof surface, use moisture meters to detect hidden saturation in decking, and evaluate flashing conditions that are impossible to assess from ground level. Schedule a professional roof inspection in Weatherford under these circumstances: after any hailstorm or wind event producing gusts above 50 mph; when your roof is 15 or more years old even without visible problems; before listing your home for sale; and whenever your DIY checklist reveals any of the issues described above.
