Roof Replacement Cost in Frisco, TX: 2026 Price Guide

What Frisco homeowners can expect to pay for a roof replacement — by material, home size, and project complexity.

March 16, 2026 SkyGuard Roofing Solutions Roofing

Frisco is among the most dynamic real estate markets in North Texas, and with home values at the levels they currently hold, a roof replacement is both a significant expense and a meaningful investment. Whether you are dealing with storm damage, an aging roof approaching the end of its useful life, or preparing a home for sale, understanding the real cost of a roof replacement in Frisco — and the variables that drive it — puts you in a much stronger position to make a sound decision. This guide provides current pricing context for the 2026 market, explains the key factors that affect your total cost, and outlines the financing and insurance avenues available to Frisco homeowners.

Average Roof Replacement Costs in Frisco, TX (2026)

Roofing costs are typically quoted per roofing square (100 square feet of roof surface), and they vary significantly by material. The ranges below reflect installed costs — material plus labor, tear-off of a single layer of existing roofing, and standard debris disposal — for a typical Frisco home with a moderately pitched roof and no unusual complexity.

Architectural Asphalt Shingles

Architectural (dimensional) shingles remain the most common choice for Frisco residential roofs. In the current DFW market, installed costs for a quality architectural shingle run approximately $4.50 to $7.00 per square foot, or $450 to $700 per roofing square. For a typical 2,000-square-foot Frisco home with a 6:12 pitch and a 25 to 30 square roof area (accounting for pitch factor), total installed cost typically falls between $11,000 and $21,000. Upgrading to Class 4 impact-resistant shingles adds roughly $0.50 to $1.25 per square foot to material costs — an investment that often pays back through insurance premium reductions within three to five years.

Metal Roofing

Standing seam steel panels and steel shingle systems carry significantly higher installed costs, typically $9.00 to $16.00 per square foot in the current Frisco market. For the same 25 to 30 square roof area, expect a total installed cost between $22,000 and $48,000 depending on material and system complexity. The higher upfront cost must be weighed against the dramatically longer lifespan — 40 to 70 years for quality steel versus 20 to 30 years for architectural shingles — and lower maintenance requirements over that period.

Concrete and Clay Tile

Tile roofing in the Frisco market runs $10.00 to $18.00 per square foot installed, with clay tile at the high end of that range. Total costs for an average home can reach $25,000 to $55,000 or more for larger homes or complex roof geometries. The structural assessment often required for tile installation adds additional cost if engineering work and framing reinforcement are needed.

Flat Roof Systems (TPO/EPDM)

For home additions or commercial buildings in Frisco, flat or low-slope roof sections using TPO or EPDM membrane systems typically run $4.00 to $8.00 per square foot installed, depending on system type and insulation requirements. Our commercial roofing team handles these projects throughout Collin County.

Factors That Affect Your Frisco Roof Replacement Cost

Roof Size and Pitch

Roof size is the primary driver of total cost — more surface area requires more material and more labor hours. Pitch (the steepness of the roof slope) adds a multiplier: a steeply pitched roof (8:12 or steeper) requires safety equipment, slower installation pace, and greater crew skill, typically adding 20 to 40 percent to base labor costs compared to a low-slope roof. Many of Frisco's newer master-planned homes feature complex rooflines with multiple pitches, valleys, hips, and dormers — all of which increase the effective square footage and installation complexity.

Number of Existing Layers

Building codes in Frisco (under City of Frisco's adopted IBC/IRC) typically permit a maximum of two layers of roofing material before a full tear-off is required. If your home already has two layers of shingles, the existing material must be removed before any new roofing is installed. Tear-off and disposal of two layers adds $0.75 to $1.50 per square foot to the total project cost versus a single-layer tear-off. If you are unsure how many layers your roof has, a pre-estimate inspection will confirm this.

Decking Condition

The roof deck — typically 7/16-inch or 5/8-inch OSB or plywood sheathing — is the substrate that everything else attaches to. When shingles are removed, any decking that is soft, delaminated, or visibly damaged must be replaced before new roofing is installed. Decking replacement is typically quoted separately at $80 to $150 per sheet (32 square feet) depending on material and local lumber pricing. On an older Frisco home, discovering 10 to 20 percent decking damage during tear-off is not unusual; on a severely water-damaged roof, it can be higher. Get a clear understanding from your contractor about how decking replacement will be handled — whether it is included in a flat estimate or billed as a variable based on what is found during tear-off.

Penetrations, Flashing, and Code Upgrades

Every roof penetration — plumbing vents, HVAC curbs, chimneys, skylights — requires flashing that must be replaced or properly integrated with the new roofing system. Many homeowners are surprised to find that code-compliant roof replacements in Frisco also require upgrades to items like drip edge installation, ice-and-water-shield coverage at eaves and valleys, and proper attic ventilation ratios. These code requirements add cost but are non-negotiable for a permitted, inspectable job. A reputable contractor will include these in the estimate and identify which items are code-required versus optional upgrades.

When to Replace vs. Repair

The repair-versus-replace decision hinges on a few key factors. If your roof is less than 15 years old, has no widespread granule loss, and the damage is limited to a discrete area (flashing failure, isolated shingle blow-off), targeted repair is almost always the right answer. If your roof is approaching 20 to 25 years old and shows widespread granule loss, multiple areas of cracking or curling, and evidence of age-related deterioration, a full replacement is typically more cost-effective than repeatedly patching an aging system. A professional roof inspection provides the data to make this decision confidently rather than guessing.

The rule of thumb often cited in the industry: if repairs will cost more than 30 to 40 percent of the cost of full replacement, replacement usually makes more financial sense — particularly on a high-value Frisco property where the cost of ongoing repairs accumulates quickly.

Insurance Coverage for Roof Replacement in Frisco

Many Frisco homeowners do not realize that their homeowner's insurance policy may cover some or all of the cost of a roof replacement if the damage is storm-related. Policies in Texas vary significantly in how they handle roofing claims — particularly around "actual cash value" versus "replacement cost value" coverage, and how depreciation is applied. Review your policy with your insurance agent before assuming your coverage position. If a recent storm has damaged your roof, SkyGuard can assist with the inspection documentation needed to support your claim. We work directly with insurance adjusters and understand the local claim process.

Financing is available for both insurance-covered and out-of-pocket projects. Ask about current terms and approval process when you request your estimate.

ROI: How a New Roof Affects Frisco Home Values

In Frisco's competitive real estate market, a new roof is a significant selling point. Real estate data consistently shows that roof replacement recovers a substantial portion of its cost in resale value — typically 60 to 70 percent in the first year, and more in high-value markets like Frisco where buyers are discerning and home inspectors flag deferred maintenance prominently. Beyond the direct dollar return, a new roof eliminates a common negotiating point that allows buyers to discount offers or demand seller-funded repairs. If you are preparing your Frisco home for sale, a proactive roof replacement can simplify the transaction and support full asking-price offers.
